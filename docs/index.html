WalkAway — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

WalkAway is a macOS menu-bar app that locks your Mac when your paired Apple Watch moves away. This policy explains exactly what the app does and does not do with your information. The short version: WalkAway collects no personal data, has no account or login, and transmits nothing off your Mac.

Data we collect

None. WalkAway does not collect, store, sell, share, or transmit any personal data. There is no account, no sign-in, no analytics, no advertising, no tracking, and no third-party SDKs.

What the app accesses on your device

  • Bluetooth signal: WalkAway reads the Bluetooth signal strength (RSSI) of the Apple Watch you select, in order to estimate proximity and decide when to lock the screen. This reading is used only on your Mac, in real time, and is never recorded or transmitted.
  • Your settings: Your preferences (selected device, lock distance, timers) are stored locally on your Mac via macOS user defaults. They never leave your device.
  • Diagnostic logs: WalkAway writes operational logs (its lock decisions and on-device performance) to Apple's local unified logging system on your Mac. These logs stay on your device, are not sent anywhere, and are not accessible to us.

Network use

WalkAway makes no network connections. It works fully offline and never contacts any server, including ours.

Permissions

WalkAway requests Bluetooth permission solely to detect the proximity of your chosen Apple Watch. It does not request access to your contacts, location, camera, microphone, photos, or files.

Children's privacy

WalkAway does not collect data from anyone, including children, and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes to this policy
